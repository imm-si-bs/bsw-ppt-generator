💡 Tips for better results
What produces good vs. bad output
✅ Good input ❌ Bad input
A full meeting transcript — even messy, unformatted notes "Make me a deck about last month"
A CSV with exact metric values, targets, and month-over-month deltas Typing numbers into the notes field without column context
"Lead with the NPS win, then process reminder, keep it under 15 slides" "Make it good" or "use your judgment"
Uploading the raw notes file and using the instruction box to say what section it belongs to Pasting everything into one box with no labels or context
"Audience is 40 global support reps — casual tone, motivating, 20-min runtime" No audience or tone context
✗ Bad prompt
"Create slides for the July support team meeting"
✓ Good prompt
"July 2026 LST Global Support Team Meeting. Audience: 40 reps across NA, EMEA, APAC, Noida. Lead with the NPS improvement (71 this month, up from 68). Remind team about new escalation policy effective Aug 1. Shout out Jane D. in EMEA for the ServiceNow migration win. Keep it upbeat — 20 min runtime."
Quick tips
  • Upload a metrics CSV — Claude reads numbers more reliably from a file than from typed values in a text box
  • Paste transcripts verbatim — Claude extracts structure from messy, unedited text; no need to clean it up first
  • Use the Section Outline field if you already know exactly what you want in a specific slide
  • Add per-file instructions after uploading — e.g. "this is the metrics data for July" or "these are transcripts from the past 4 weeks"
  • More context is always better — there is no such thing as too much source material
  • Review every slide before distributing — placeholder slides will include speaker notes explaining what to fill in
